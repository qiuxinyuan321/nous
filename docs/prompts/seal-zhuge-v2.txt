A single traditional Chinese carved stone seal imprint, photographed in pure isolation, centered on a completely clean pure white background.

The seal contains EXACTLY ONE Chinese character, and that character MUST be 亮 (pinyin: liàng, meaning "bright, clear"). This is a classical Han / Qin dynasty small seal script (秦汉小篆) rendering.

STRUCTURE of the character 亮 in small seal script — you must render these three vertical zones top-to-bottom, in this exact order:
  • TOP zone: 亠 radical — one short horizontal stroke at the very top, sometimes with a small dot above it.
  • MIDDLE zone: 口 — a small rectangular square shape.
  • BOTTOM zone: 儿 — two vertical legs splaying slightly outward, like a pair of stretched brackets standing on the ground.
Total: three distinct stacked elements, vertically composed, top-heavy-to-bottom rhythm.

CRITICAL — what the character is NOT:
  • NOT 壽 (longevity) — do NOT render horizontal ladder-like stacked lines
  • NOT 喜 (joy) — do NOT put a 口 at the bottom
  • NOT 寿 / 帝 / 亭 / 高 — these have different stroke structures
  • NOT any other character — only 亮
