A single traditional Chinese carved stone seal imprint, photographed in pure isolation, centered on a completely clean pure white background.

The seal contains EXACTLY ONE Chinese character, and that character MUST be 楚 (pinyin: chǔ, meaning "clarity, state of Chu, distinct"). This is a classical Han / Qin dynasty small seal script (秦汉小篆) rendering.

STRUCTURE of the character 楚 in small seal script — you must render these two vertical zones top-to-bottom, in this exact order:
  • TOP zone: 林 radical — TWO copies of the 木 (tree) radical placed side by side. Each 木 in small seal script looks like a central vertical stem with two curved branches going up-left and up-right near the top, and two curved roots going down-left and down-right near the bottom. The two trees are placed symmetrically side-by-side, sharing the top register.
  • BOTTOM zone: 疋 radical — a small cap at the top (like a flattened arc), below which are two curving symmetrical strokes that splay outward and down like a pair of bent legs, with a small vertical drop in the middle.
Total: TWO tree radicals on top, one 疋 foot radical beneath. Top-to-bottom layered structure.

CRITICAL — what the character is NOT:
  • NOT 林 alone (two trees only) — there must be a 疋 beneath
  • NOT 森 (three trees) — ONLY TWO trees on top
  • NOT 丹 / 己 / anything resembling 丹丹己
  • NOT any other character — only 楚
